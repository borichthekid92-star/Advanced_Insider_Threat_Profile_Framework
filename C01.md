# Corporate espionage agent

## Description
This profile details an insider who has been recruited, coerced, or self-motivated to conduct espionage on behalf of a competitor, foreign entity, or external criminal organization. Unlike the disgruntled employee, this actor may show no visible signs of dissatisfaction. They are patient, methodical, and often highly trusted within the organization. Their primary objective is the silent, sustained extraction of intellectual property, trade secrets, strategic plans, or sensitive personnel data for the benefit of an external party.
Psychology

Psychology: The corporate spy is often motivated by financial incentive, ideological alignment with a foreign government, coercion through blackmail or threat, or extreme career ambition. Because emotional grievance is absent, behavioral indicators are subtler than other malicious insider types. This actor may compartmentalize stress effectively, maintain strong social relationships at work, and perform well on the job as cover. Over time, small behavioral tells may emerge — unexplained wealth, unusual travel patterns, overly curious interest in departments outside their own, or abnormal after-hours system access. The threat is compounded by long dwell times; this actor may operate undetected for months or years.

## Example scenario
A senior R&D engineer at BioCore Pharma is quietly approached via LinkedIn by a recruiter linked to a foreign state-sponsored firm. Offered financial compensation, the engineer agrees to share ongoing drug trial data. Over eight months, they access clinical trial repositories using their legitimate credentials during normal work hours to avoid anomaly detection. They use a personal laptop connected to a coffee shop hotspot to upload compressed, password-protected archives to an encrypted cloud drop. They deliberately stay under DLP file-size thresholds, use decoy filenames, and rotate exfiltration timing monthly. The activity surfaces only when a peer notices the engineer photographing a whiteboard presentation during a closed strategy session.

### TA001Preparation
The actor establishes external contact with a recruiting party, negotiates terms, and surveys the organization's data landscape using legitimate access. May research internal org charts, identify high-value data owners, and download reference documentation to understand what is most valuable to the external party.
Techniques
External recruiter contact via personal device
Internal org chart reconnaissance
GitHub/internal wiki research
Identification of IP-rich teams
Target data asset mapping
Initial test data sample collection

Detection

Unusual interest in departments outside own role
Personal device use for work research
Encrypted messaging app installation on workstation
LinkedIn profile changes to obscure employer

### TA002 Access abuse
The actor leverages legitimate credentials, elevated permissions, or socially engineered access to reach sensitive repositories and data stores beyond normal job scope. May seek to harvest colleague credentials to obscure attribution, or exploit trusted relationships to access shared accounts.
